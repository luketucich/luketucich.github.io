Website Evaluations

CRAP Design Principles Analysis

Stripe

Stripe is basically the go-to payment processor for online businesses. Their website does double duty - showing off their product and helping developers figure out how to actually use it. It's clean, modern, and actually makes payment processing look kinda cool.

  • Contrast: Stripe absolutely nails contrast. They keep it simple with white backgrounds and dark text, then pop in that signature purple color to make important stuff stand out. Everything's super readable, and you can actually tell what's clickable without squinting.
  • Repetition: They're super consistent across the whole site. Same fonts everywhere, same button styles, same purple color popping up in all the right places. It feels like one cohesive experience instead of a bunch of random pages thrown together.
  • Alignment: Everything lines up perfectly. It's like they used an invisible grid system and actually stuck to it. No wonky spacing or weird positioning - everything feels intentional and organized. It's the kind of site that just looks professional.
  • Proximity: They group related stuff together really well. Navigation stays in one spot, content sections are clearly separated, and there's enough white space so nothing feels cramped. You can scan the page and immediately understand what's what.

Pacific Northwest X-Ray

Pacific Northwest X-Ray sells medical equipment, mostly X-ray machines and stuff like that. Their website is supposed to help doctors and hospitals find what they need, but honestly it's a bit of a mess. It's like they built it in 2005 and never updated it.

  • Contrast: This site is a contrast disaster. They've got red text on blue backgrounds, yellow text that's basically invisible on white, and busy patterns that make everything harder to read. It's like they picked colors out of a hat and went with it.
  • Repetition: There's zero consistency here. Every page looks different - different fonts, different colors, different layouts. The navigation changes from page to page, and there's no real branding to speak of. It's like they let everyone design their own page.
  • Alignment: Nothing lines up properly. Text blocks are all over the place, images are crooked, and the navigation looks like it was thrown together by someone who's never used a computer before. It's chaotic and honestly kind of stressful to look at.
  • Proximity: Related stuff is scattered everywhere. Navigation links are hiding in random places, unrelated content is mixed together, and there's no clear organization. You have to hunt through a bunch of random information just to find what you're looking for. It's exhausting.
